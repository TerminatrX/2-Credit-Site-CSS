About Us

Computer Science Society (CSS) is a resource that provides an opportunity for students at DePaul University to collaborate on computer science-related projects, expand their credit outside the classroom, and to join a network of hardworking people both in school and in the field.

Each week at CSS we try to do something different, whether it's going for a company site visit, having a faculty member speak about their research, or doing some interview prep. The goal of CSS is to help bridge the gap between the classroom and the real world.

Meetings are held from 5PM-5:45PM on Wednesdays in room 350 in the CDM building. All are welcome!

As an organization, we go to a bunch of events and sometimes change meeting times, so to stay up to date, join our Facebook Group and our Slack Channel.

2-Credit Course



Through CSS, we off a 2-credit project course that allows students to take what they have learned in class and apply it to a project of their interest.


The class has three main requirements: you learn something new or expand on your current knowledge, you work on a full-scale project either alone or with a team.

You present your project at the CSS meeting during week 10. Every couple weeks throughout the quarter, there will be a check-in just to make sure progress is being made.


Enrollment for the course starts week 1 and goes until week 3 of each quarter. For questions about enrollment in the course or the course in general, please email us at cssdepaul@gmail.com.
